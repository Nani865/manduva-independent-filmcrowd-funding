Synopsis

In a quiet village, a father and son run a small toddy business at a local brewing spot called Manduva. The father, a hardworking climber, extracts toddy from palm trees, while the son reluctantly sells it, dreaming of a different life. When the son asks for a bike, the father agrees—but only if he helps with the business, forcing the young man into a routine he resents.

One day, their world turns upside down when a dead body is discovered at Manduva. The villagers immediately blame the toddy, but the father and son know there's more to the story. As suspicion grows, the son, a movie fanatic, begins using his cinematic knowledge to investigate the mystery.

Amid the chaos, the protagonist forms a close bond with a 10-year-old boy who often visits Manduva to watch movies with him. The boy, quiet yet observant, finds comfort in their friendship, escaping his troubled home where his alcoholic father torments his mother.

As the protagonist pieces together the truth, he stumbles upon a shocking revelation—he unknowingly killed the victim. But the real mastermind is someone he never suspected. The boy, in a desperate act of revenge, manipulated the protagonist into poisoning his own father using a toxic plant from Manduva.

Faced with the weight of this revelation, the protagonist is left at a crossroads: Will he expose the truth and risk everything, or live with the guilt of a crime he never intended to commit?
